The September Offer Strategy
01
Volume
Apply to 30–50 startups in Aug–Sep. Not big companies yet. Target 0–2 yr experience roles only. Use LinkedIn Easy Apply + startup job boards (AngelList, Cutshort, Internshala).
02
Proof Pack
IPL Kaggle notebook + Power BI dashboard + 1 LinkedIn post = your Sep portfolio. That combination is enough. Don't wait for the perfect project before applying.
03
Your Angle
Position as "The analyst who can communicate." Most data freshers can't write. You can. Sahitya + SEO + storytelling is your differentiator. Lead with it.
Daily Time Budget
You don't need 8 hours a day. You need 2.5 hours — consistently. Here is exactly how to split it.
Daily Schedule — 2.5 Hours
Block 1 · 45 min
SQL / Python / Core Skill Learning
Structured learning only. A course module, a tutorial, or problem-solving. No passive YouTube rabbit holes.
Block 2 · 45 min
Active Project Work
IPL → BPL → E-Commerce → OTT. Build the deliverable. Write code. Build the dashboard. This is where the proof of work gets made.
Block 3 · 30 min
Reading / Strategy / Writing
Blogs, teardowns, case studies. Lenny's Newsletter, Reforge, Mixpanel blog. No passive watching. Read → summarise in 3 lines.
Block 4 · 30 min
LinkedIn + Portfolio Output
1 post per week minimum. Screenshot your work. Write 3 lines of analysis. Post it. Your LinkedIn is your live, compounding portfolio.
Weekly Output Targets
□1 SQL problem solved (easy / medium)
□1 notebook or dashboard update pushed to GitHub
□1 LinkedIn post — analysis, teardown, or insight
□1 blog or article read + 3-line summary written
□1 job application sent (from Aug onwards)
What NOT to Do
✗Don't learn everything before applying
✗Don't do 10 courses without 1 finished project
✗Don't skip LinkedIn — it's your live portfolio
✗Don't apply to MNCs in the first wave
✗Don't wait for "the perfect project" to post
Your Unfair Advantage
You write well (SEO + Sahitya). You think in systems (community building). You love sports (genuine domain knowledge). Most data freshers are pure data people with zero communication skills. You are building both. That combination — analytics + storytelling — is rare and highly hireable. The goal isn't to become a data scientist. It's to become the analyst who tells the best story with data.
